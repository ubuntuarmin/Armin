Methodology

Theory & Equations

This calculator applies the Kirsch analytical solution with Mohr-Coulomb and tensile failure criteria using effective (poroelastic) stresses.

Kirsch Equations

Stresses at the wellbore wall (r = rw) are computed from the Kirsch (1898) solution for a circular hole in a biaxial stress field, extended here to deviated wells via full tensor rotation.

σθθ = (σx' + σy') − 2(σx' − σy')cos2θ − 4τxy' sin2θ − Pw σrr = Pw σzz = σz' − 2ν[(σx'−σy')cos2θ + 2τxy' sin2θ]

Effective Stresses

Biot's poroelastic theory converts total stresses to effective stresses, controlling rock failure. α = 1 is the most conservative assumption.

σ'ij = σij − α·Pp σ'rr = Pw − α·Pp σ'θθ = σθθ − α·Pp σ'zz = σzz − α·Pp

Mohr-Coulomb (Collapse)

Shear failure (wellbore breakout) occurs when the maximum principal effective stress exceeds the Mohr-Coulomb criterion. q = tan²(45 + φ/2).

Failure: σ'₁ = UCS + q·σ'₃ Pw_collapse = (3σH − σh + α·Pp(q−1) − UCS) ÷ (1 + q) where q = (1+sinφ)/(1−sinφ)

Tensile Fracture

Tensile fractures initiate when the minimum effective hoop stress equals the negative of the rock's tensile strength. For a vertical well this occurs at θ = 0° (σH direction).

Failure: σ'θθ_min = −T₀ Pw_frac = 3σh − σH − α·Pp + T₀ Fracture gradient = Pw_frac / TVD

Stress Tensor Rotation

For deviated wells, in-situ stresses are transformed from the global frame (σv, σH, σh) to the wellbore frame using full 3D tensor rotation.

σx' = σH cos²i cos²α + σh cos²i sin²α + σv sin²i σy' = σH sin²α + σh cos²α σz' = σH sin²i cos²α + σh sin²i sin²α + σv cos²i τxy' = −(cosi/2)sin2α·(σH−σh)

Mud Weight Window

The safe drilling fluid density (mud weight) window lies between the collapse and fracture pressures, constrained below by pore pressure to prevent kicks.

Lower bound (EMW): max(Pp, Pw_collapse) / (0.052·TVD_ft) Upper bound (EMW): Pw_frac / (0.052·TVD_ft) Window = Upper − Lower [ppg]
