Methodology

Theory & Equations

Kirsch analytical solution with 3-D tensor rotation, Biot effective stresses, Mohr-Coulomb collapse, and coupled tensile fracture criterion.

3-D Stress Tensor Rotation

Far-field stresses (σv, σH, σh) are rotated from global to wellbore frame via direction-cosine tensor using inclination i and azimuth α.

σx' = σH cos²i cos²α + σh cos²i sin²α + σv sin²i σy' = σH sin²α + σh cos²α σz' = σH sin²i cos²α + σh sin²i sin²α + σv cos²i τxy' = −½ cos i · sin 2α · (σH − σh) τxz' = +½ sin 2i · (σH cos²α + σh sin²α − σv) τyz' = −½ sin i · sin 2α · (σH − σh)

Kirsch Wall Stresses

For a circular borehole (pressure Pw) the 1898 Kirsch solution (extended to 3-D) gives stresses at r = rw.

σ'rr = Pw − αPp σ'θθ(θ) = (σx'+σy') − 2(σx'−σy')cos2θ − 4τxy'sin2θ − (Pw−αPp) σ'zz(θ) = σz' − 2ν[(σx'−σy')cos2θ + 2τxy'sin2θ] − αPp τ'θz(θ) = 2(−τxz'sinθ + τyz'cosθ)

Principal Stresses & MC Criterion

σ'rr is always principal at the wall (τrθ=τrz=0). The θ-z plane gives σ'A and σ'B by eigenvector analysis.

σ'A,B = ½(σ'θθ+σ'zz) ± √[((σ'θθ−σ'zz)/2)² + τ'θz²] Sort: σ'1 ≥ σ'2 ≥ σ'3 MC Failure: σ'1 = UCS + q·σ'3 q = (1+sinφ)/(1−sinφ) c = UCS(1−sinφ)/(2cosφ)

Collapse — Newton-Raphson

At each angle θ, solve for Pw where σ'A(Pw) = UCS + q·σ'rr(Pw). 720-point scan; maximum Pw = required collapse pressure.

f(P) = ½(C1−P+szz_e)+R − UCS − q·P = 0 R = √[((C1−P−szz_e)/2)² + τθz²] df/dP = −½ − half_diff/(2R) − q where half_diff = (C1−P−szz_e)/2 Collapsed over all θ → Pw_collapse

Fracture — Closed Form

Set minimum principal stress σ'B = −T₀ at each θ and solve analytically, accounting for τ'θz coupling.

Pw_frac(θ) = C1(θ) + T₀ − τ'θz(θ)² / (σ'zz(θ) + T₀) + α·Pp (Reduces to C1+T₀+αPp when τθz=0) Min over all θ = hydraulic frac. pressure

Safe Window & Breakout

The window spans max(Pp, Pw_collapse) to Pw_frac. The breakout halfwidth is the arc of wellbore circumference in MC failure at planned MW.

MW_min = max(Pp, Pw_collapse) / (0.052·TVD_ft) MW_max = Pw_frac / (0.052·TVD_ft) Breakout at angle θ if: σ'1(θ,Pw_planned) ≥ UCS + q·σ'3 Breakout halfwidth θbo = arc fraction × 90°
