Welcome to The Kitchen

Where every bowl is crafted with intention — small batches, human-grade protocols, and a kitchen you'd be proud to eat from yourself.

Small-Batch Philosophy

Quality You Can Taste, Because We Never Rush It

Mass production is the enemy of quality. When a company makes thousands of pounds of food at a time, corners get cut — on ingredients, on cooking temperatures, on quality checks.

At The Artisan Bowl, we cook in small, carefully monitored batches. This means we can personally inspect every ingredient before it goes in, control the exact cooking temperature and duration, and never sacrifice flavor or nutrition for volume.

"We could make ten times the food. We choose not to. Small is how we stay honest."

Our Process

From Farm to Bowl — Every Step Matters

1
🛒

Ingredient Sourcing

We source proteins from local and regional farms that raise animals without antibiotics or hormones. Vegetables come from trusted local growers.

2
🔍

Incoming Inspection

Every delivery is visually inspected before entering our kitchen. We reject anything that doesn't meet our freshness and quality standards.

3
🍲

Slow Cooking

Proteins and vegetables are cooked at precise temperatures to retain maximum nutrition while ensuring complete food safety compliance.

4
🌡️

Temperature Testing

Each batch is temperature-tested before packaging to verify it has reached safe internal temperatures — the same standard as human food facilities.

5
📦

Portioning & Packaging

Food is portioned into sealed, food-grade containers. We label every container with the batch date, ingredients, and a best-by date.

6
❄️

Cold Storage & Delivery

Orders are stored in refrigerated conditions and delivered fresh within 24 hours of preparation — right to your door in Katy.

7
🧹

Sanitation Protocol

Our kitchen is cleaned and sanitized between every batch using food-grade, pet-safe cleaning agents — documented in our cleaning log.

8
🐾

Happy Dog, Happy You

The final step is always your dog's reaction. We consider a clean bowl within 30 seconds our highest quality metric.

Food Safety

We Follow Human-Grade Food Safety Protocols

The phrase "pet food grade" has always bothered us. Why should safety standards be lower for your family member? We hold ourselves to the same standards as a commercial human kitchen.

🌡️

HACCP Principles

We follow Hazard Analysis and Critical Control Points (HACCP) methodology — the same framework used in FDA-regulated human food facilities — to identify and control potential food safety hazards.

🧴

NSF-Certified Cleaning

All surfaces, equipment, and utensils are cleaned with NSF-certified food-safe sanitizers. No harsh chemicals that could transfer to your dog's food. Cleaning logs are maintained for every session.

🧤

Personal Protective Practices

All food handlers wear nitrile gloves, hair covers, and clean aprons while cooking. Handwashing occurs before, during, and after food preparation — no exceptions.

📋

Batch Traceability

Every batch receives a unique lot number that traces ingredients back to their source. If there's ever a quality concern, we can identify and recall affected products within hours.

❄️

Cold Chain Management

From the moment ingredients arrive to the moment your order is delivered, temperature is continuously monitored. The cold chain is never broken — ever.

🔬

No Cross-Contamination

Different proteins are prepared on separate, color-coded cutting boards and in dedicated cookware. We eliminate cross-contamination risk the same way allergy-aware restaurants do.

The Human-Grade Difference

Good Enough for You to Eat — Seriously

We use the phrase "human-grade" because it's accurate — not as marketing. Every ingredient in our kitchen is sourced from human food supply chains, handled under human food safety protocols, and prepared in a manner consistent with human food production.

In fact, our staff joke that the food smells so good it's tempting to try it. A few have. They survived. (In all seriousness — yes, you could technically eat your dog's bowl. We'd suggest grabbing your own lunch though.)

This standard costs us more time and more money. We do it anyway because it's the right thing to do.
