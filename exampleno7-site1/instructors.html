Meet Your Instructors

Our instructors are working artists and dedicated teachers who bring both technical mastery and genuine enthusiasm to every class.

At Beyond the Canvas Art Studio, Farnaz Amin brings over 25 years of teaching experience to every class. Small class sizes mean you receive real, personalised guidance — not just a demonstration from the front of the room.

👩‍🎨
Farnaz Amin
Studio Founder & Lead Art Instructor
Art School Graduate 25+ Years Teaching International Exhibiting Artist

Born in Los Angeles to an Iranian family, Farnaz Amin has been immersed in Art since childhood. Trained in multiple countries and a graduate of Art school, she has exhibited her work internationally and won several awards. She founded Beyond the Canvas Art Studio & Gallery to share her passion for Art with all ages — creating a space where anyone can explore, grow, and find their creative voice.

Daniel Park
Head of Ceramics
BFA Ceramics, SAIC 9 Years Teaching Production Potter

Daniel is a production potter and ceramics teacher with nearly a decade of experience. He trained at the School of the Art Institute of Chicago and spent two years in residency in Korea studying traditional Korean celadon techniques. His teaching style is deeply hands-on — he'll shape clay alongside you, not just watch. Daniel's pots are sold in craft galleries across the country.

Our Approach

Teaching Philosophy:
Progress Over Perfection

We believe everyone has creative potential — it just needs space to grow. Our instructors don't teach a single "correct" way to make art. They teach you to see, observe, and respond — and then get out of your way.

👁️
Learning to See
Before you can paint or draw well, you need to truly observe. We slow students down and train their visual perception.
🔄
Iteration Over Outcome
Mistakes are data. We celebrate experiments, encourage iteration, and treat every failure as a lesson.
🌱
Nurturing Voice
Technical skill is a tool, not a destination. We help students develop a personal artistic identity they can build on forever.

"Art isn't just a skill — it's a way of life. I've had students who walked in convinced they couldn't draw, and within a few weeks they were making work they were genuinely proud of. The hand follows the eye, and confidence follows the hand."
